### SECTION 3: Product & Technology (MOST IMPORTANT SECTION)

**Q: Describe your product in 1-2 sentences**
> AP Sachivalayam AI Copilot is a Telugu-first WhatsApp AI assistant for Andhra Pradesh's 1.3 lakh village secretariat employees — it instantly answers scheme eligibility queries across 100+ government schemes, auto-fills official forms from voice/text input, tracks citizen grievances with 72-hour SLA enforcement, and AI-prioritizes daily tasks across 34 departments to reduce employee burnout.

**Q: What problem are you solving?**
> AP's 1.3 lakh village secretariat employees each manage 34 departments and 100+ welfare schemes for 5 crore citizens. The 2025 Yogandhra survey exposed that staff are under severe pressure — answering scheme questions from memory, manually typing forms for hours, with no grievance tracking or task management system. The AP government called for AI tools for real-time monitoring of secretariats. Every existing AI product (Jugalbandi, Sarvam AI) targets citizens — nobody has built for the employees who actually process citizen requests.

**Q: What AI/ML technologies do you use?**
> Claude API (Anthropic) for reasoning, drafting, and structured extraction. OpenAI Whisper large-v3 for Telugu speech-to-text. Sentence-transformers with pgvector for RAG-based scheme knowledge retrieval. Rapidfuzz for Telugu fuzzy matching. Built on FastAPI with PostgreSQL, Redis, and Celery. Production-ready: 45+ API endpoints, 270 passing tests, 70 source files, Docker deployment.

**Q: What is your unfair advantage / moat?**
> Three moats: (1) AP-specific domain knowledge — 30+ scheme data files with Telugu eligibility criteria, GO references, FAQs that no generic AI has. (2) GSWS portal integration — we're designed as a plugin to the government's existing infrastructure, not a replacement. (3) Zero competition — every GovTech AI targets citizens; we're the only product for the 1.3L government employees who serve them.

**Q: What traction do you have?**
> Production-ready codebase: 45+ API endpoints, 270 tests, 12 WhatsApp conversation intents, 30+ scheme databases, 10 form templates, 9 Telugu prompt templates. Pilot playbook ready for 5 secretariats in 1 district. Applying to IndiaAI Innovation Challenge (AP governance track) and planning direct RTGS pilot proposal. GitHub: https://github.com/jyothikutti15-jpg/AP-sachivalayam

**Q: Which focus area best describes your startup?**
> Select: **Sovereign AI** (Telugu-first, AP-specific localized AI)
> Secondary: **Agentic AI** (grievance workflows, task prioritization, SLA enforcement)

### SECTION 4: Technical Challenges

**Q: What are your top technical challenges?**
> 1. Scaling WhatsApp-based AI to 1.3 lakh concurrent users across 11,162 secretariats with varying connectivity (some villages have intermittent internet — we built offline fallback but need infrastructure for state-wide scale).
> 2. Cost optimization — currently using Claude for all LLM tasks. Need to route simple Telugu queries to a cheaper model (evaluating Gemini as alternative) while keeping Claude for complex reasoning.
> 3. Real-time GSWS portal integration — the government portal has rate limits and downtime. Need robust retry/queue architecture for reliable form submission at scale.

---

### SECTION 5: How Google Can Help

**Q: How can Google's expertise help your startup?**
> 1. Google Cloud infrastructure for deploying to 11,162 secretariats across AP — we need managed PostgreSQL, Redis, and Kubernetes for state-wide scale.
> 2. Gemini API access to evaluate as a cost-effective LLM alternative for simple Telugu queries (greetings, FAQs) while keeping Claude for complex reasoning — reducing our per-query cost by 60-70%.
> 3. Android team mentorship for integrating our copilot into the existing GSWS Employee App (which every secretariat employee already has on their phone).
> 4. Google Cloud TPU access for fine-tuning a Telugu-specific model on our 30+ scheme knowledge base.
